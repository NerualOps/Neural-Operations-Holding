About NeuralOps

Creating standalone AI that attaches to its own CRM—one complete system

The Founder

I started coding when I was about 15 years old, and I fell in love with it instantly. I coded cheats for video games and built many websites, but nothing captured my interest quite like artificial intelligence.

I started working on this project about 4 months ago, and I'm loving every single step of the journey. My plan is to grow this into a company that makes AI actually useful for real work, not just marketing hype.

Epsilon AI represents everything I believe AI should be: self-contained, actually intelligent, and genuinely helpful. It's not a chatbot wrapper around someone else's API—it's a real AI model that we continue to train and improve. We're actively working on expanding its capabilities and building the infrastructure to make it even more useful for real business work.

What is Epsilon AI?

Epsilon AI is a standalone AI assistant built from the ground up. Unlike other AI solutions that rely on external APIs, Epsilon AI runs entirely on your infrastructure. It's a transformer-based language model that we continue to train and improve. We're actively working on expanding its capabilities to better understand your business and help you work more efficiently.

Our plan is to build Epsilon AI into a complete system that attaches to its own CRM. It's not just a chatbot—we're developing it to understand your business processes and provide genuine assistance. We're continuously training the model on better data and refining its capabilities.

What makes Epsilon AI different is our commitment to continuous improvement. We don't just deploy a static model—we're actively training it, refining it, and building the infrastructure to make it more useful. Our goal is to create an AI that genuinely understands your business and helps you work better, not just one that searches documents or quotes training data.

How We're Training Epsilon AI

1. Continuous Model Training

We're continuously training Epsilon AI on better data to improve its capabilities. We collect training data from various sources, refine it, and use it to train new versions of the model. Our goal is to make Epsilon AI better at understanding business contexts, communication, and providing helpful assistance.

2. Document Processing Pipeline

We're building systems to process documents and extract useful information that can be used for training. This includes understanding business documents, sales materials, and knowledge bases. The processed data helps us train the model to better understand your business context.

3. Feedback Integration

We collect feedback from users about what works and what doesn't. This feedback helps us identify areas where the model needs improvement, and we use it to create better training data for future model versions. Your input directly shapes how we train Epsilon AI.

4. Future Plans

Our plan is to build automated training pipelines that can continuously improve the model based on new data and feedback. We're working toward systems that can evaluate model performance, identify improvement areas, and generate better training data automatically. This is a work in progress, and we're building it step by step.

Where Epsilon AI Is Right Now

Epsilon AI is fully functional and we're actively working on improving it. Here's what we have now:

  • Transformer-based language model - Fully trained and generating responses
  • Continuous training - We're actively training new versions of the model with better data
  • Document processing - Systems to process and understand business documents
  • Feedback collection - Gathering user feedback to improve future training
  • Model deployment - Infrastructure to deploy improved model versions

We're continuously working on Epsilon AI. Every version we train is better than the last. We're building the systems and infrastructure to make it more useful for real business work. It's not a static system—we're actively developing it, training it, and improving it every day.

Business Automation
